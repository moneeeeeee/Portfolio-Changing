Spinal Cord Scaffold – CNT-MSC Implant for Neural Regeneration

Biomedical Design Contributor | ISO 10993-5, ethics and regulatory analysis, cost models

Overview

I proposed a fibrin scaffold with carbon nanotubes and autologous MSCs to bridge spinal cord injuries. The design focused on biocompatibility, durability, and axon regrowth with an eye on global access.

Tools

  • Literature review and benchmark criteria
  • ISO 10993-5 cytotoxicity standards
  • Health Canada CTA pathway and GMP considerations
  • Bioprinting cost models and deployment planning

Role

Biomedical Design Contributor

Description

I defined criteria for scaffold biocompatibility and mechanical targets and mapped a development path from in vitro validation to clinical trials. I created a low-cost fabrication model using adapted 3D printers and outlined partnerships for scalable manufacturing. I addressed ethics, consent, and privacy using PIPA-aligned practices. The work links engineering design with regulatory readiness and access.
